Teaching Assistant, GCSE Tutor

Beckfoot School

For the 2021-22 academic year, I tutored Year 11 students in Maths, English, and Science, helping them prepare for their GCSE exams. Each week, we tackled areas they found difficult, re-exploring them in ways they found engaging and informative.
To accelerate my internal progress into becoming an effective, approachable tutor, I focussed my efforts to devloping learning strategies for weaker students specifically, giving me a strong insight into tackling 'maths anxiety', and providing inspirational 'lightbulb' moment: seeing a tutee's excitement as they grasped these (previously foreign) topics motivated me to go above and beyond to ensure that student's success - creating personalized revision plans in my free time, and making regular progress check-ups.
This role was deeply fulfilling to me, and significantly sharpened my interpersonal communication and patience.
In the summer of 2025, I worked with students of diverse abilities under a teacher training placement, challenging me to re-frame my approach to explaining topics in such a way to engage each class.
Demonstrating my confidence and eagerness to learn, I was asked to lead a full A-level Maths lesson independently, for whcih I made an exciting yet informative lesson plan on series convergence (extending the pre-existing spec to introduce Ramanujan summation and Zeno paradoxes). The level of student engagement I received from this lesson exceeded my high expectations, reinforcing my ideals of inspiring students via creative teaching.
I spent a large amount of the experience taking notes and seeking feedback from experienced teachers, constantly reflecting on their methods and unique styles, then channelling this back into my own teaching mindset. This experience was instrumental in building my confidence and resilience in a classroom setting, paving the way for future opportunities.

Chess Club Founder & Leader

Beckfoot School

With nothing but a small portion of the school budget, I founded a school chess club, and (over the next year) grew it into a weekly event that regularly drew in 70+ young people.
During this process, I hired volunteers to help organise the club, spent time each week planning & setting up for each session, and gave students one-on-one (or group) support, where I taught them how to develop their chess ability.
Represents a concrete legacy at Beckfoot School: since leaving for university, the club has continued to grow and expand, with the core infastructure I founded giving way to new and exciting opportunities.
For example, in February 2023, I took initiative of our finances to purchase the resources necessary (eg: chess clocks) to establish and run a tournament structure. In recent years, this format and equipment have been used to host regional tournaments, giving members the opportunity of representing their school by playing chess!
This task required immense organisation & confidence, along with a constant positive attitude and adaptability.
